Title: Intracellular Protein Transport and Membrane Interaction System

Summary:
The system of proteins analyzed in this study is primarily involved in intracellular protein transport and interactions with various cellular membranes. The majority of the proteins are found in the cytosol, intracellular membrane-bounded organelles, and the plasma membrane. Key processes performed by this system include protein binding, intracellular protein transport, and interactions with endosomes, Golgi apparatus, and endoplasmic reticulum. The system is proposed to be named the "Intracellular Protein Transport and Membrane Interaction System" to reflect its primary functions and locations within the cell. A critical goal of the analysis is to determine if this is a novel complex or if any proteins are novel members of a known complex.
