The system of proteins analyzed in this study is primarily involved in mitochondrial ribosome and protein binding functions. The majority of the proteins are localized in the mitochondrion, with a significant number also involved in the mitochondrial inner membrane, mitochondrial translation, and mitochondrial large ribosomal subunit. The system plays a crucial role in various biological processes such as translation, RNA binding, and structural constituent of ribosome. Given the functions and locations of the proteins, we propose the name "Mitochondrial Ribosome and Protein Binding Complex" for this system. 1. Greber, B. J., & Ban, N. (2016).
